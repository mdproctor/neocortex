---
title: Central Bank Holds Interest Rates Steady
domain: news
tags: economy, rates, monetary-policy
---

The Federal Reserve maintained its benchmark interest rate range at 5.25 to 5.50 percent following this week's policy meeting, signaling continued vigilance against persistent inflation despite signs of economic cooling. The decision marks the third consecutive meeting without a rate adjustment, as policymakers assess whether prior rate hikes have sufficiently dampened price pressures.

Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell emphasized that the central bank remains data-dependent and will adjust policy as conditions warrant. Recent inflation readings show progress toward the Fed's 2 percent target, but core inflation remains elevated, particularly in services excluding housing. Labor market data presents a mixed picture: unemployment has ticked up to 4.1 percent while wage growth remains above historical averages.

Bond markets reacted modestly to the announcement, with the 10-year Treasury yield rising 7 basis points to 4.28 percent as investors recalibrated expectations for future rate cuts. Equity markets advanced on the news, with major indexes gaining between 0.5 and 1.2 percent.

The Fed's updated economic projections suggest one or two rate cuts may be appropriate later this year if inflation continues to moderate, though officials stressed that the timing and magnitude of any cuts will depend entirely on incoming economic data rather than predetermined schedules.
