---
title: Employment Notice Period Requirements
domain: legal
tags: employment, notice, termination
---

Either Employee or Employer may terminate this employment relationship upon provision of written notice, with the required notice period determined by Employee's length of continuous service as follows: for service of less than two (2) years, two (2) weeks' notice; for service of two (2) years but less than five (5) years, four (4) weeks' notice; for service of five (5) years or more, eight (8) weeks' notice. Notice periods commence on the date written notice is delivered to the other party.

Employer may elect to place Employee on garden leave during all or part of the notice period. During garden leave, Employee shall remain employed and continue to receive full salary and benefits, but shall not be required to perform duties or attend the workplace. Employee shall remain bound by confidentiality, non-solicitation, and other restrictive covenants during garden leave. Employer may revoke garden leave status at any time, requiring Employee to resume normal duties with forty-eight (48) hours' written notice.

Alternatively, Employer may elect to terminate employment immediately upon payment in lieu of notice. Payment in lieu shall equal Employee's base salary for the applicable notice period, calculated on the basis of Employee's regular working hours as of the termination date. Payment in lieu does not include variable compensation, bonuses, or equity awards, which shall be treated in accordance with the terms of the applicable plans.

Notice requirements do not apply to termination for cause as defined in Section 9 (Termination for Cause).
